Decimal Pentatonic Scale: 
The Harmonic Order of $1000$ Hz This project defines a new musical scale 
derived from the marriage of the Decimal System and Just Intonation. 
By setting the fundamental frequency at exactly $1000$ Hz, we achieve 
a pentatonic structure where every note exists in a state of mathematical
purity, free from the "beating" (interference) inherent in 12-tone equal
temperament (12-TET).1. The PhilosophyStandard musical tuning ($A = 440$ Hz)
is a social convention. The Decimal Pentatonic Scale is a mathematical 
necessity.By choosing $c = 1000$ Hz, we align the perception of sound
with the base-10 logic of our physical and digital reality. 
We discard the irrational numbers of equal temperament in favor of 
clean integer ratios.2. Technical SpecificationsThe scale consists of 
five notes (Pentatonic) within a single octave, defined by their 
relationship to the

While labeled as "eflat" for convenience, the frequency of $1250$ Hz
($5/4$) represents a mathematically perfect Major Third, providing a 
unique "blue" tonal quality when played within this decimal context.
3. Mathematical EleganceThe beauty of this scale is revealed when expressed as 
a series of integers. The entire scale can be represented by the following ratio:
$$12 : 15 : 16 : 18 : 20 : 24$$This sequence avoids the complexity of modern tuning, 
returning to a "Pythagorean" ideal where harmony is a direct reflection of whole numbers.
4. Why Pentatonic?The 5-tone restriction is intentional. By removing the "leading tones" 
and tritone-forming notes of a full 7-tone scale, we eliminate dissonance. 
Every combination of these five frequencies results in a stable, crystalline 
resonance.5. Potential ApplicationsPure Tone Synthesis: Ideal for testing the limits of human perception using pure sine waves.Ambient/Generative Music: Creating soundscapes that feel "static" and "ordered."Mathematical Education: 
Visualizing the relationship between frequency, decimals, and geometry (Lissajous curves).
